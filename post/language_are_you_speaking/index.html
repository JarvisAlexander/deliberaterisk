What Language Are You Speaking?

14 Oct 2016, 15:50

Before I start exploring the concepts or risk, I need address how I define commonly used jargon. Essentially, I want to create a risk vocabulary in which readers can more clearly understand my work based on how I am using specific words. In most instances, this will not be an issue. My goal is to make my writing clear and concise. I will strive to use simpler words and shorter sentences. However, there are certainty commonly used jargon that I won’t be able to avoid.

In general, any terms that I use will follow the definition presented in the CFA Glossary. Throughout my writing I will refer various CFA articles and quote relevant CFA publications when explaining financial concepts. Mainly because I am a CFA charter holder and a vast majority of my fundamental knowledge in the area of risk management comes from the study material that I used while I was a CFA candidate. But also because the CFA institute and its members have consistently demonstrated a high level of expertise in various financial subjects and this has translated into the production of well regarded research and publications.

Instead of constantly linking back to the CFA Glossary, as I continue to write on various subjects, when there are certain words or concepts that I either frequently reference or have become the basis to important discussions I will define them on the “Risk Vocabulary” page.

Similar to all of my work on this blog, the Risk Vocabulary page is a work in progress. My hope is that it will always be forward progress.
